About the exhibition

Lovelace, the dawn of computing

Dive into the visionary world of the pioneer who designed the first algorithm in history through an immersive experience combining science, history, and innovation.

EXHIBITION'S TEASER

A PLAN FOR FUTURE

In 1842, Ada Lovelace translated an article on Babbage's Analytical Engine and added her own notes. Her "Note G" contained an algorithm for calculating Bernoulli numbers, recognized as the first computer program in history.

Babbage's Analytical Engine was the first programmable computer. Never built in its time, it inspired Alan Turing a century later to develop his Universal Machine, the foundation of modern computing.

Our circular exhibition invites you on an immersive journey into the world of Ada Lovelace. Explore her life and work in time, from her adolescence in Victorian England to the impact of her ideas on modern computing.
