ADA LOVELACE

Machine Analytique

  • Ada Lovelace est créditée d'avoir écrit le premier algorithme pour la machine, faisant d'elle la première programmeuse informatique au monde et une figure clé de son héritage.
  • La Machine Analytique a été la première machine à proposer l'utilisation d'un programme stocké, posant ainsi les bases des concepts modernes de l'informatique.
  • Conçu par Charles Babbage dans les années 1830, la Machine Analytique est considéré comme le premier concept de machine informatique à usage général mécanique.

Impact

"Je suis plus que jamais l'épouse de la science. La religion, pour moi, c'est la science, et la science est la religion." Ada Lovelace

1843 – The First Algorithm

"The first steps towards modern computing."

  • Ada Lovelace writes notes on Babbage’s Analytical Engine and describes the first algorithm, making her the world’s first programmer.
  • She envisions that machines could manipulate not just numbers, but symbols and even create music—an early vision of AI!

1936 – Alan Turing and the Universal Machine

"A theory that would change everything."

  • Mathematician Alan Turing develops the concept of the Turing Machine, which is considered the foundation of modern computers.
  • He studied Lovelace’s work and even referenced her in his famous essay on AI, where he discussed the “Lovelace Objection” (the idea that machines can’t create independently).

1940s – The Birth of Modern Computers

"From theory to reality."

  • The first electronic computers are built (ENIAC, Colossus).
  • Programming languages start to emerge, laying the foundation for modern coding.

1950s-60s – The Rise of Programming Languages

"A world built on code. "

  • The first high-level programming languages are created: FORTRAN, COBOL, LISP.
  • Many early programmers were women, following in Lovelace’s footsteps.

1970s-80s – Personal Computers & Software Boom

"Bringing computers to everyone."

  • Companies like Apple and Microsoft make computers accessible to individuals.
  • Ada, a programming language named after Lovelace, is created by the U.S. Department of Defense.

2000s-Present – AI, Algorithms, and Lovelace’s Vision

"Machines composing music, just as she imagined."

  • Artificial Intelligence (AI) advances, fulfilling Lovelace’s 1843 prediction that computers could be used for more than just math.
  • Ada Lovelace is now widely recognized as a pioneer of computer science, with annual Ada Lovelace Day celebrating women in STEM.
